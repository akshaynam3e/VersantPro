📢 Section 1 — Repeat Sentence 20 pts
🎯
The AI scores every single word, not just meaning
Unlike a human examiner who accepts paraphrasing, the Versant AI compares your spoken words to the original sentence word-by-word. Replacing "approved" with "accepted" costs you marks even though the meaning is similar. Focus on exact repetition.
👂
Audio plays only once — do not blink during it
The sentence plays once at normal speed. Do not try to write it down — there's no time. Instead, listen with full attention, then immediately repeat it out loud. The gap between the audio ending and your speaking should be under 2 seconds.
🗣️
Articles (the, a, an) matter — do not drop them
Many candidates drop articles when repeating. "Manager approved proposal" instead of "The manager approved the proposal" will lose 1–2 marks per sentence. Train yourself to include every article.
🔊
Speak at the same pace as the original sentence
If the AI-spoken sentence was slow and clear, repeat it at the same pace. Rushing causes the speech recognition to miss words. If it was faster, match that energy. Pace matching = higher fluency score.
💬 Section 2 — Conversation 18 pts
📌
Answer the question — not the conversation
You hear two people talk, then a question is asked. The question is about a specific detail (time, number, name, place). Listen for those details specifically. Example: if the question is "What time will she arrive?" your answer must include the exact time mentioned.
⏱️
You have 15 seconds to answer — use a full sentence
Don't just say "One o'clock". Say "She will arrive at one o'clock." Full sentences score higher on grammar and fluency. One-word answers get partial marks at best.
🧠
If you missed the detail, make a logical guess
Silence scores zero. If you didn't catch the specific answer, say a related sentence like "I believe the meeting was scheduled for the afternoon." Fluent, relevant speech with wrong detail scores better than silence.
✏️ Section 3 — Fill in Blanks 14 pts
📝
Only one word is accepted — not a phrase
The blank always expects a single specific word. "five engineers" when the blank wants "five" will be marked wrong. Read the sentence structure carefully — the surrounding grammar tells you what type of word is needed (number, time, day, name).
⚡
You have 25 seconds — speed matters here
This section is pure reading comprehension speed. Read the full sentence first, identify what's missing logically, then type. Don't overthink — your first instinct is usually correct. Practice typing quickly without typos.
🔢
Numbers, times, and days are the most common blanks
Most blanks test whether you understood a number (five, ten, two), a time (Monday, evening, January), or a specific noun from context. Train your ear to catch these details when listening to the conversation section — they overlap.
🎧 Section 4 — Dictation 14 pts
👂
Listen to the full sentence before typing a single word
The biggest mistake in dictation: starting to type while the sentence is still playing. You miss the last 3–4 words every time. Let the sentence finish completely, hold it in short-term memory, then type it all at once. This takes practice but it's the correct technique.
✅
Spelling errors within 2 characters still earn partial marks
The Versant AI uses a spelling tolerance system. "recieve" instead of "receive" or "scheduld" instead of "scheduled" may still earn partial credit. However, completely wrong words score zero. Focus on getting the right word, correct spelling is secondary.
🗑️
Don't add or remove words — even filler words count
Adding "please" or removing "all" from the sentence will be penalised. The AI compares word-for-word. Type only what you heard, nothing more, nothing less. Avoid punctuation guessing — it varies by test version.
📖 Section 5 — Retell Paragraph 14 pts
🧩
The AI checks meaning, not exact words
This section is different from dictation. You are not expected to copy the paragraph. The AI evaluates whether you understood the key facts, the sequence of events, and the conclusion. Use your own words — it scores higher than a word-for-word copy.
📌
Identify 3 key facts during reading time
During the 30-second reading window, focus on: (1) Who is involved, (2) What happened / what was the problem, (3) What was the result. If your written retelling covers these three, you will score 10–14 points reliably.
✍️
Write at least 3–4 complete sentences
One-sentence retellings score very low on clarity. Aim for 3–5 sentences that flow logically. Start with the main character or situation, describe what happened, and end with the outcome. Example structure: Setup → Problem → Resolution.
⏰
You have 90 seconds to write — don't waste the first 20
Many candidates stare at the blank screen for 15–20 seconds thinking. Instead, start writing immediately once the paragraph disappears. Even rough sentences can be improved, but you can't recover lost time. Write first, edit if time allows.
⚡ Quick Reference — Do's & Don'ts

✅ Always Do This

  • Speak immediately after audio ends
  • Use full sentences in answers
  • Include articles (the, a, an)
  • Listen fully before typing
  • Write 3+ sentences in retell
  • Speak at natural conversational pace
  • Stay calm — confidence affects fluency
  • Practice every section daily

❌ Never Do This

  • Type while audio is still playing
  • Skip/stay silent — always attempt
  • Speak too fast trying to finish
  • Paraphrase in Repeat Sentence
  • Copy paragraph word-for-word in Retell
  • Add extra words in dictation
  • Answer before the question is asked
  • Practice only one section
🤖 How the Versant AI Scoring Engine Works

What the AI Actually Measures

  • Phoneme accuracy — each sound you produce is matched against a phonetic model of standard English
  • Word error rate — percentage of words in your response that don't match the expected output
  • Response latency — how quickly you begin speaking after the prompt ends (longer delays reduce fluency score)
  • Sentence completeness — did you finish the sentence or trail off
  • Semantic relevance (Conversation + Retell) — keyword and meaning overlap with correct answer

Common Misconceptions

  • Speaking louder does NOT help — clarity matters, not volume
  • Indian accent is fine — the AI is trained on multiple English accents including Indian English
  • Grammar mistakes in Repeat Sentence don't matter — word matching does
  • You cannot re-attempt any audio — there is no replay button in the real exam
